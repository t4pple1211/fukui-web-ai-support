# MICHELL DESIGN

## Mission

Create a modern, memorable marketing website for MICHELL.

The design must communicate the excitement of a hamburger food truck and encourage visitors to imagine MICHELL at their event.

Prioritize emotion before information.

Visitors should immediately feel:

- Fun
- Street
- Festival
- Freshly cooked
- Local
- Authentic

# Design Philosophy

The website should feel like discovering a popular food truck at a local festival.

It should be energetic without becoming chaotic.

The design should rely on:

- Typography
- Composition
- Motion
- Color contrast
- White space

rather than decorative graphics.

Every section should feel alive.

Every important action should feel inviting.

---

# Visual Direction

The visual language should combine:

- Street culture
- Festival atmosphere
- Craft burger shop
- Food truck
- Youth culture

Reference mood:

- Human Made
- Stüssy
- Supreme
- Nike
- Blue Bottle Coffee
- ONIBUS COFFEE

Do not imitate these brands.

Create an original visual identity inspired by their confidence and simplicity.

# Typography

Bebas Neue

Japanese

Zen Kaku Gothic New

Rules

English display text should feel bold and expressive.

Japanese should feel clean, modern and highly readable.

Prefer spacing and typography hierarchy over oversized text.

Large headlines should create impact through composition.

---

# Shapes

Rounded corners.

Sticker-inspired labels.

Bold geometric blocks.

Simple graphic accents.

Avoid excessive decoration.

---

# Motion

Motion should feel natural and premium.

Avoid flashy animations.

Prefer:

- reveal animations
- stagger animations
- floating elements
- subtle parallax
- smooth hover interactions

Motion should guide attention.

Motion should never distract.

Support:

prefers-reduced-motion

---

# Components

Buttons should feel tactile.

Cards should have generous spacing.

Navigation should remain simple.

Interactive elements should clearly communicate hover, active and focus states.

Every important action should attract attention without overwhelming the page.

---

# Imagery

Photography may not always be available.

The design must still feel complete using:

- logo
- typography
- illustrations
- icons
- geometric graphics
- stickers
- event atmosphere

Avoid generic stock photography.

Layouts should be designed so that real photography can be added later without redesigning the page.

---

# Layout Principles

Use strong visual hierarchy.

Create rhythm with alternating calm and energetic sections.

Use generous spacing.

Do not fill every area with decoration.

Guide the visitor naturally toward the primary action.

---

# Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.2 AA.

Maintain sufficient contrast.

Support keyboard navigation.

Maintain visible focus states.

Use semantic HTML.

Support reduced motion.

---

# Responsive Design

Design mobile-first.

Large touch targets.

Comfortable spacing.

Sticky mobile CTA.

Fast loading.

Avoid unnecessary JavaScript.
